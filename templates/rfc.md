# RFC Template

> Use an RFC for an open-ended proposal where the right approach is genuinely undecided — unlike an
> ADR (`adr/template.md`), which records a decision **already made**. An RFC that reaches consensus
> on an irreversible or cross-Engine decision graduates into an ADR via `prompts/create-adr.md`;
> the RFC stays as the historical record of the discussion, and the ADR becomes the binding
> decision.

## Problem

*What's genuinely unresolved, and why it matters enough to warrant a broader discussion rather than
one person just deciding.*

## Proposal

*The author's actual recommendation — an RFC without a recommendation just describes a problem,
which isn't enough. State a real position, even if you expect it to be challenged.*

## Alternatives Considered

*Other approaches, and their trade-offs, stated fairly enough that a reader could disagree with the
Proposal in an informed way.*

## Impact

*What this touches if accepted — which Engine(s) (`.ai/architecture.md` §2), whether it implies a
contract change, whether it needs a glossary update (`.ai/constitution.md` Article VI).*

## Open Questions

*Specific things this RFC does not yet resolve, addressed to specific people/roles if known.*

## Resolution

*Filled in once discussion concludes: what was decided, by whom, and — if the decision meets the
ADR bar (`.ai/development-principles.md` §2) — a link to the resulting ADR.*
