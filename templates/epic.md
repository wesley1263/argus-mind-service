# Epic Template

> Use this to group several related features/specs under one larger initiative — when a goal is too
> big for one spec but its pieces need to stay visibly connected. An epic tracks and links; it does
> not itself contain Acceptance Criteria or an API section — those live in the constituent specs.

- **Owner:**
- **Status:** Proposed | Active | Completed | Abandoned
- **Date opened / target timeframe:**

## Goal

*The outcome this epic exists to deliver, stated at the level a stakeholder would recognize — not
a list of features. One or two sentences.*

## Business Driver

*Why this, why now — the constraint, opportunity, or commitment that makes this epic real rather
than aspirational.*

## Constituent Specs

*Every spec under this epic, and its current status. Update this list as specs are added — don't
let it go stale.*

| Spec | Status | Owning Engine(s) |
|---|---|---|
| `specs/<name>.md` | Draft / Approved / In Progress / Implemented | … |

## Success Metrics

*How you'll know this epic delivered its Goal — specific and measurable where possible, not "users
will be happier."*

## Out of Scope

*What this epic deliberately does not include, so a constituent spec doesn't silently grow beyond
what was actually agreed.*

## Risks / Dependencies

*Anything that could block or invalidate this epic — a dependency on another epic, an unresolved
architectural question that needs `prompts/review-architecture.md` first, a resourcing constraint.*
